2020-10-13
Mastering the Ability to be Alone
Quiet? feels familiar from the first, even like a friend .... really likes that. but why now even become afraid and even hide from it. I don't know, what's wrong with me, maybe the loneliness I feel now is different from the loneliness it used to be.

I realize that the current loneliness is more because I want to reach the top of Mount A and not all of my friends / colleagues want to reach the same mountain (I hope, you know what I mean), it’s a sense of loneliness now.

Isn't there one person or several people who have the same goal?

of course there is! because actually all friends are in the same camp, but not all of them want to move to get to the top, as if it looks like natural selection, but not explicitly. sounds funny huh? haha, but it’s the choice of each individual, I have no right to sue them. and from there there are fewer and fewer 'participants' participating in the event. and yes, this loneliness is getting closer.

Let’s get over some of the cowardly sheep who are comfortable in the camp, ‘cause I have an interesting subject than that.
After I saw and heard about Mastering the Ability to be Alone, it was explained that feeling lonely when we want to reach the top of a mountain is natural.
and about friends slowly leaving us? of course, they also discussed it.
Is it natural? It is reasonable! I repeat it again N A T U R A L !

For those of you who are feeling the same way, calm down! you are not alone I also feel that way. It’s quiet, there are no friends to accompany you, but believing it all will pay off, it’s just not easy.
Be patient and consistent with what you are pursuing / working on, see you above!
